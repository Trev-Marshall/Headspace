State Sponsored Saving Investments

What exactly is a 592 Savings Plan?
A 529 Savings Plan is an educational investment plan that allows tax breaks for educational based expenses. Similar to a Roth IRA, but with education in mind, the plan is a tax advantaged savings account focused on encouraging and easing future educational plans.
What are the benefits associated with a 529 account?
As your savings compound over a period of time, withdrawls associated with qualified educational expenses will not be subjeted to tax. This differs from a typical savings account where withdrawls are deemeda s capital gains and are thus taxed.
What can my savings be spent on?
Savings from the 529 plan can only be spent on qualified education expenses. There is a 10% tax penalty on unqualified noneducational expenses.

Education and Finance

What if my child doesn't go to college?
To avoid any taxes or penalties on withdrawls, your best option is to transfer the account to a new beneficiary who will use the money for educational expenses. Otherwise, the gains on any withdrawls from the account will be subjected to taxes and a 10% penalty.
Can I use the funds to pay for k-12 school tuition?
While education accounts were originally created for college expenses, it's also possible to use the money for K-12 tuition. Qualified educational expenses include books, room and board, computer equipment, and tuition.

Financial Terminology 101

Assets: anything that is worth some value to a person, company, or organization

Beneficiary: a person who receives money as a result of something such as a trust fund, will , or life insurance

Custodial Account: a person or organization responsible for mamaging money or property for another person or organizatio

Fiduciary: a person or organization responsible for managing money or propert for another person or organization

Financial Portfolio: the collection of all of your financial assets, such as stocks, bonds, 529 plans, etc...

Fund: money that is set aside for a specific purpose (ie. investments)

Investment: money allocated with the expectation of generating value overtime

Trust: a fidicuary arrangement that allows a third party, or trustee, to hold assets on behalf of a beneficiary

Uniform Gifts to Minors Act (UGMA): a type of custodial account that allows transfer of assets, such as an investment, to a minor without requiring a trust
